Getting started

  1. Keeping track of your cash flows
    • If you are employed by a registered employer, you can get either a W-2 or a W-4 form from them
    • Alternatively, if you are self employed or have income not from a job, use a 1099 form
    • When doing this, make sure that you have a good account of all the money you have earned and have good records


  2. Reporting Cash flows
    • Option 1 is to claim that the money is gift from uncle
    • Alternatively, create a shell business and pay yourself as an equity dividend, not as a wage so you avoid self employment tax
    • If you want to avoid taxes, try to maximize payments in cash, and only report the cash flows into your back account to the IRS
    • This way, you don't get taxed on your cash earnings since they aren't traceable
    • to legally reduce taxes, make sure you deduct interest expenses and depreciation from earnings first, you get a tax break


  3. Making sure you don't get caught (very important)
    • If you use the method of not reporting cash, don't make the mistake of depositing it all into your bank account after in large sums
    • This will alert the IRS that you have unreported income, and looks very suspicious
    • If you suspect that the IRS is after you, move your money into a swiss bank account
    • You can also consider moving to Lithuania or Thailand where the IRS will have less direct influence
    • May need to take alternative transportation such as a boat or private plane, as you could get caught at the airport unless you have fake id
    • In extreme cases, it may be favorable to first fake your death to keep them off of your tail
